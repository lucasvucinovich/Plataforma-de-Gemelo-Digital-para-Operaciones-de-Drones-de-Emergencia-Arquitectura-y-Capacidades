# HPE CDS Tech Challenge — Fase II. Conflicto

**Challenge brief for BMAD brainstorming and planning.**  
Use this document as the single source of context for the agent and the team.

---

## 1. Scenario: Horizonte Cero — Realidad Simulada

After the partial reactivation of the **HPE GreenLake Alliance** and the recovery of critical nodes in Spain, Colombia, and Mexico, cross-data analysis, network patterns, and covert movements revealed:

- **The threat was not only outside. The threat was inside.**

For months, infiltrated members of **"La Sombra" (The Shadow)** have operated from within the Alliance, using legitimate infrastructure, real data, and critical systems to design a **large-scale, coordinated attack** capable of:

- Collapsing entire cities  
- Paralyzing emergency services in a matter of minutes  

Evidence points to an **unprecedented scenario**:

- Multiple simultaneous incidents  
- Resource saturation  
- Critical decisions made under pressure  
- No margin for error  

**Core directive:** The Alliance needs to **anticipate**. Reacting is no longer enough: **the future must be simulated before it happens.**

---

## 2. The Alliance’s Response

For the first time, the HPE GreenLake Alliance is activating a **predictive defense program** based on:

- Data  
- Artificial intelligence  
- Advanced computing  

**Mission:** Create **operational Digital Twins of emergency service vehicles** that:

- Reproduce the real behavior of the systems  
- Analyze critical scenarios  
- Anticipate failures  
- Optimize decisions **before** the threat materializes  

*“Aquí comienza vuestra misión.”* — Here begins your mission.

---

## 3. Your Task in Phase II

You have been selected to take the first step.

**Deliverable:** Design and build a **functional prototype (POC)** of a **Digital Twin** of an **emergency service vehicle** that:

- Simulates its behavior in **critical situations**  
- Delivers **operational intelligence** to the Alliance  

**Vehicle type is your choice**, for example:

- Police vehicle  
- Ambulance  
- Fire truck  
- Civil protection unit  
- Hybrid or specialized vehicle  

**Important:** The focus is **not** the vehicle itself, but **your digital twin’s ability to anticipate, analyze, and support decision-making.**

---

## 4. Prototype Capabilities (Minimum)

The prototype must be capable, **at least**, of:

| Capability | Description |
|------------|-------------|
| **State representation** | Represent the state of the vehicle from real or simulated data |
| **Telemetry & events** | Process telemetry and events |
| **Anomaly & risk detection** | Detect anomalies, risks, or critical situations |
| **Scenario simulation** | Allow scenario simulation (“what if…”) |
| **Actionable insights** | Provide actionable insights based on data |

---

## 5. Artificial Intelligence (Examples)

AI can be incorporated in multiple ways, for example:

- **Fault prediction**  
- **Anomaly detection**  
- **Resource usage optimization**  
- **Decision-making assistance**  

---

## 6. Design Philosophy: Ecosystem-Ready

- The digital twin **must not** be limited to an isolated representation of a single vehicle. It **must be designed to integrate** into a broader digital ecosystem (other twins, digitized environment). That full ecosystem does **not** need to be implemented in this phase.

- Teams must define **conceptually and technically**:
  - What types of **events** the twin receives from the environment  
  - What **information** it exchanges with other twins  
  - How it **reacts** to those stimuli  

- **Focus:** Conceive a digital twin **prepared to communicate, coordinate, and scale** within an intelligent network of interconnected assets — not to build the complete ecosystem.

## 9. Summary

- **Problem:** Internal threat (“La Sombra”) has used Alliance infrastructure to plan a coordinated attack that could collapse cities and paralyze emergency services. The Alliance must **anticipate** by **simulating the future** before it happens.  
- **Solution:** A **Digital Twin POC** of an emergency vehicle that represents state (real/simulated), processes telemetry and events, detects anomalies/risks, supports “what-if” simulation, and provides actionable insights; designed for future integration into a network of twins and digitized environment.  
- **Success criteria:** Anticipation, analysis, and decision support — not the vehicle type. Ecosystem-ready design (interfaces, events, behavior with other twins) must be defined conceptually and technically.  
- **Outputs:** One PDF (technical doc + embedded links to video and repo/prototype), one video (≤5 min), optional POC with code; **deadline 8 March 2025, 23:59 CET.**
